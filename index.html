Why the Rankings Collapsed — Root Cause Analysis

🎯 Most Likely Root Causes (Algorithm-Based)

  • Thin, templated city/service pages: Site has multiple near-identical location pages (Redwood City water heater, San Carlos water heater, Burlingame trenchless, etc.) — a primary Helpful Content Update target since 2023
  • Google January 2026 Core Update: Timeline matches exactly — gradual drop beginning Jan 10 aligns with Google's core update rollout window. Sites with thin local landing pages were heavily impacted
  • Google March 2026 Core Update: Likely compounded the January damage — sites that didn't recover between updates often lose further ground
  • Low E-E-A-T signals: Plumbing is a "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL) category — Google requires strong expertise, experience, authority, and trust signals. Missing: author bios, license numbers, certifications, review schema
  • Internal keyword cannibalization: Multiple pages targeting the same service in the same city (e.g., trenchless repair Redwood City appearing across several URLs) splits authority and confuses Google about which page to rank

✅ Confirmed: Site IS Indexed (Not De-Indexed)

  • site:yourbellplumbing.com shows 90+ pages in Google's index — pages exist but rank on pages 8–11+
  • The GSC near-zero impressions are because pages rank positions 200–2,000,000+, not because they're missing from the index
  • This is actually harder to fix than a noindex bug — ranking recovery requires content quality improvements, not just a technical toggle
  • Check GSC Manual Actions tab to rule out a manual spam penalty (would accelerate recovery timeline if confirmed clean)
  • Check GSC Coverage for any crawl errors or soft 404s that may be compounding the issue
  • Verify internal linking structure — thin location pages need stronger internal link signals to compete
📊 The gradual collapse pattern (not overnight) is the fingerprint of a Google Core Update — NOT a technical issue like robots.txt or noindex.

Core updates roll out over 1–2 weeks, which explains why impressions dropped from 1,900/day (Jan 9) → 661 (Jan 15) → 120 (Jan 31) progressively. A technical block would cause an overnight drop to zero. The recovery path is content quality improvement, not a technical fix.
