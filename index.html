Why the Rankings Collapsed: Root Cause Analysis

🎯 Most Likely Root Causes (Algorithm-Based)

  • Thin, templated city/service pages: Site has multiple near-identical location pages (Redwood City water heater, San Carlos water heater, Burlingame trenchless, etc.) — a primary Helpful Content Update target since 2023
  • Google January 2026 Core Update: The timeline lines up exactly. The gradual drop starting January 10 matches Google's core update rollout window. Sites with thin local landing pages were heavily impacted
  • Google March 2026 Core Update: Likely made the January damage worse. Sites that were already struggling often drop further
  • Low E-E-A-T signals: Plumbing is a "Your Money or Your Life" category, so Google holds it to a higher standard. Right now the site is missing key trust signals like author bios, license numbers, certifications, and review schema
  • Keyword cannibalization: Multiple pages targeting the same service in the same city (for example, trenchless repair Redwood City appears across multiple URLs. That splits the ranking authority and makes Google unsure which page to show

✅ Confirmed: Site IS Indexed (Not De-Indexed)

  • site:yourbellplumbing.com shows 90+ pages in Google's index, but they rank on pages 8–11+
  • The near-zero impressions in GSC are because pages are ranking around position 200–2,000,000+, not because they are missing from the index
  • This is honestly harder to fix than a simple noindex bug. Recovering from a ranking collapse takes content quality improvements, not just flipping a switch
  • Check the GSC Manual Actions tab to rule out a manual spam penalty. If it is clean, that actually speeds up the recovery path
  • Also check GSC Coverage for crawl errors or soft 404s that could be making things worse
  • Review the internal linking structure. Thin location pages need stronger internal links to have any chance of competing
📊 The gradual collapse pattern (not overnight) is a clear sign of a Google Core Update, not a technical issue like robots.txt or noindex.

Core updates roll out over 1–2 weeks. That explains why impressions went from 1,900 per day on January 9, to 661 by January 15, to 120 by January 31. A technical block would have dropped everything to zero overnight. The fix here is content quality, not a technical tweak.
